<p>The immune system protects the body against disease or other potentially damaging foreign bodies. When functioning properly, the immune system identifies and attacks a variety of threats, including viruses, bacteria and parasites, while distinguishing them from the bodys own healthy tissue.</p><div id="ad-unit-1" class="ad-unit"></div><p> </p><aside class="hawk-placeholder" data-render-type="fte" data-skip="dealsy" data-widget-type="seasonal"></aside><p>The Lymphatic system consists of bone marrow, spleen, thymus and lymph nodes. </p><p> </p><p>Bone marrow produces white blood cells, or leukocytes.</p><div id="ad-unit-2" class="ad-unit"></div><p> </p><p>The spleen is the largest lymphatic organ in the body contains white blood cells that fight infection or disease.</p><p> </p><div class="mid__article"></div><p>The thymus is where T-cells mature. T-cells help destroy infected or cancerous cells.</p><div id="in-article" class="ad-unit"></div><p> </p><p>Lymph nodes produce and store cells that fight infection and disease.</p><p> </p><p>Lymphocytes and leukocytes are small white blood cells that play a large role in defending the body against disease.</p><p> </p><p>The two types of lymphocytes are B-cells, which make antibodies that attack bacteria and toxins, and T-cells, which help destroy infected or cancerous cells.</p><p> </p><p>Leukocytes are white blood cells that identify and eliminate pathogens .</p><p><strong>Related</strong>:</p><ul><li><a href="https://www.livescience.com/26579-immune-system.html" data-url="/26579-immune-system.html">Immune System: Diseases, Disorders &amp; Function</a></li></ul>
